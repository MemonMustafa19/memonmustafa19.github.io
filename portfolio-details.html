Critical Analysis of WIL Placement

Describe

During my WIL placement at Australian Community Media (ACM), I was responsible for key tasks that enhanced my technical and transferable skills. These tasks included developing and maintaining Apache Airflow Directed Acyclic Graphs (DAGs) to automate data workflows, executing SQL queries in Google BigQuery, and participating in the data migration from Universal Analytics (UA) to Google Analytics 4 (GA4). Additionally, I contributed to the Churn Prediction project and other general bug fixes and feature enhancements, involving data engineering and analysis. A significant task was the migration of data from UA to GA4, which required creating SQL queries to extract, transform, and load (ETL) data efficiently while ensuring data integrity. Optimizing these queries to handle large datasets exceeding 3 TB was crucial for cost-effective computation. Another important task was the Churn Prediction project, which involved data preparation, feature engineering, and applying machine learning algorithms to develop predictive models for customer churn.

Interpret

This placement allowed me to deepen my understanding of data engineering and analytics. Working on the UA to GA4 migration required an in-depth understanding of both the legacy and new analytics systems. Ensuring data integrity during the migration process emphasized the importance of accurate data handling. I developed robust ETL pipeline that processed large volumes of data efficiently, a critical skill in the data analytics industry. The Churn Prediction project provided significant learning opportunities in data engineering. Integrating diverse data sources like subscription data, Google Analytics data, and Braintree transaction data provided comprehensive experience. Developing a baseline machine learning model using XGBoost and experimenting with other algorithms like Random Forest gave me practical insights into model selection and evaluation. This project highlighted the importance of thorough data preparation and feature engineering.

Evaluate

Reflecting on my actions, I realize that while I demonstrated strong technical skills, there were areas for improvement. For example, during the initial stages of the UA to GA4 migration, I encountered difficulties in optimizing query performance due to my limited experience with BigQuery. Seeking advice from experienced colleagues and consulting documentation improved my efficiency. This highlighted the importance of proactive learning and seeking help when necessary. Additionally, my time management skills could have been better. Juggling multiple projects sometimes led to tight deadlines and increased stress. In future placements, I plan to implement more effective time management strategies, such as prioritizing tasks based on their impact and deadlines, to ensure a balanced workload. On the other hand, my collaboration skills were a significant strength. Regular meetings with my manager and external teams helped me understand project requirements and deliverables clearly. My adaptability and problem-solving skills were evident when handling unexpected issues, such as resolving data discrepancies for the churn project and fixing a major bug in my GA4 pipeline. These experiences reinforced the value of effective communication and teamwork. Furthermore, mandatory cybersecurity training sessions taught me how to protect sensitive data and adhere to industry best practices. This training not only improved my technical knowledge but also heightened my awareness of ethical considerations in data handling.

Plan

Moving forward, I plan to build on these experiences by focusing on continuous learning and professional development. I aim to enhance my data optimization skills by taking advanced courses in BigQuery and other data management tools. Additionally, I will improve my project management skills by learning more about agile methodologies and gaining certifications like the Certified ScrumMaster (CSM). To remain adaptable and capable of handling future challenges, I will commit to regular self-assessment and reflection. This involves setting specific, measurable goals for skill development and seeking feedback from peers and mentors. I also plan to stay updated with the latest advancements in data analytics and machine learning by participating in workshops and conferences. Engaging with professional communities through platforms like LinkedIn and attending industry events will help me stay abreast of new trends and network with other professionals.

Conclusion

My WIL placement at ACM was a transformative experience that significantly enhanced both my technical and transferable skills. By reflecting critically on my actions and outcomes, I have identified areas for improvement and developed a clear plan for continuous learning and professional growth. This commitment to lifelong learning will not only enhance my employability but also ensure my success in the dynamic field of data analytics and backend development.

Evidence of Technical and Transferable Skills/Knowledge

Artefact 1: Creating and Optimizing Data Pipelines with Python and SQL

Context: During my placement at Australian Community Media (ACM), I was assigned to create and optimize one of the key data pipelines in preparation for the transition from Universal Analytics (UA) to Google Analytics 4 (GA4). This project was crucial because UA, a widely used analytics platform, was being deprecated, and the company needed to ensure continuity in tracking and analysing user behaviour. The data collected is vital for strategic decision-making, marketing, and improving user experience on digital platforms.

Action: To achieve this, I developed and maintained Apache Airflow Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) to automate these data workflows. My role involved writing and optimizing SQL queries in Google BigQuery to handle over 3 TB of data efficiently. Initially, I conducted a thorough review of existing data pipelines to identify bottlenecks and inefficiencies. I then implemented enhancements, such as optimizing SQL queries for better performance and restructuring data storage to reduce costs. Additionally, I designed and implemented new pipeline to manage the GA4 data, ensuring that data ingestion, transformation, and loading processes were seamless and robust.

Result: The optimized queries and automated pipeline resulted in an 23% reduction in daily pipeline runtime and a 30% reduction in costs associated with querying. These improvements not only enhanced system performance but also ensured timely availability of accurate data for analytics purposes. This allowed the marketing and analytics teams to make data-driven decisions more effectively, thereby improving campaign targeting and overall business strategies. Additionally, the transition to GA4 was smooth, with no significant data loss or downtime, maintaining the integrity and continuity of data analysis.

Learning: Through this process, I significantly enhanced my technical skills in Python programming and SQL. I gained extensive hands-on experience with Airflow and BigQuery, which are critical tools in modern data engineering. I also learned about cloud data management, including techniques for optimizing query performance and managing large datasets efficiently. Furthermore, this project underscored the importance of thorough planning and continuous monitoring in data engineering projects. I leveraged my prior knowledge in Python, gained during my coursework, to optimize the data transformation scripts, ensuring they were both efficient and maintainable. The skills I developed during this placement are directly applicable to my career in data analytics, where efficient data processing and automation are essential. This experience has also emphasized the need for continuous learning and adaptability in the fast-evolving field of data science and analytics.

Evidence of Awareness of Ethical Conduct

Reflection on Ethical Conduct in the ICT Industry During WIL Placement

During my Work Integrated Learning (WIL) placement at Australian Community Media, I encountered situations that emphasized the importance of ethical conduct and professional accountability in the ICT industry. One significant instance involved handling sensitive user data while optimizing data pipelines using Apache Airflow and Google BigQuery.

Professionalism and Ethical Considerations

In my role, I was responsible for optimizing queries and managing data workflows involving large datasets with potentially sensitive information. This responsibility highlighted the ethical considerations of data privacy and security. Misuse or mishandling of personal data can lead to severe consequences for users and the organization. To address this, I ensured all personal information was anonymized and accessed only through secure, authorized channels, adhering strictly to data encryption and storage protocols.

Proper Use of Personal Information

While working on the churn prediction project, which involved developing a machine learning model to predict customer churn, I handled sensitive subscription and transaction data. I made sure to follow best practices for data security, including anonymization and secure access protocols, to prevent any breaches of confidentiality.

Ethical Use of AI

In developing a machine learning model for churn prediction, integrating diverse data sources such as subscription details and user engagement metrics, ethical use of AI was crucial. I ensured the model was transparent and avoided biases that could unfairly target user groups. Communicating the model's potential implications and limitations to stakeholders was key to maintaining ethical standards.

Responsible Adoption of Disruptive Technologies

During the data migration from UA to GA4, maintaining data integrity and compliance with regulations was a priority. Thorough testing and validation processes were followed to ensure the transition did not compromise data security or ethical standards.

Areas for Improvement

While I managed these situations with professionalism, continuous learning in cybersecurity is essential. Proactively engaging with the cybersecurity team to stay updated on emerging threats could have enhanced my approach. Additionally, fostering a culture of responsibility by encouraging open discussions about ethical dilemmas within the team can further strengthen our commitment to ethical conduct.

Conclusion

In summary, my WIL placement underscored the critical importance of ethical conduct and professional accountability in the ICT industry. By adhering to data privacy best practices, using AI responsibly, and striving for continuous improvement, I aimed to uphold the highest standards of professionalism and ethical responsibility.
